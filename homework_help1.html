What causes thunderstorms?
Step 1: Warm, moist air rises – Thunderstorms typically form when warm, moist air rises rapidly.
Step 2: Formation of clouds – As the air rises, it cools and condenses into clouds, often forming cumulonimbus clouds.
Step 3: Electric charge build-up – Within the clouds, positive and negative charges separate, eventually leading to lightning and thunder.
